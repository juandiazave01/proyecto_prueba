FIELD ARCHIVE

Systems & equipment

Two areas I work in most. Pick one to see its process stages.

A typical nonwoven line turns raw fiber into finished roll goods across a few key stages: opening and blending the fiber, forming the web, bonding it, and winding the finished material. These are the systems most central to keeping that process running.

Fiber opening & blending

Raw fiber is opened and blended before it ever reaches the line.

Web forming

Carding or airlaid systems lay the fiber into a uniform web.

Thermal bonding

Calenders or ovens bond the web into a stable material.

Winding & packaging

Finished material is wound into rolls and prepared for shipping.

Line 4 is the production line I've spent the most time on — from web forming through drying, calendering, collection, and packaging. Here's a look at its major process stages.

Carding / airlaid forming

Dryer & calender

Sets the web's final bond and finish before collection.

Collection & transport

Moves finished material from the line to the next process step.

Packaging & palletizing

Final packaging stage before product leaves the line.
